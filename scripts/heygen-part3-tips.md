Here are the tips that separate beginners from power users. First, use the voice cloning feature. Upload a one minute recording of your voice and HeyGen creates videos that sound exactly like you. This makes the content feel authentic even though you're not on camera. Most people skip this and their videos sound robotic.

Second, master the script formatting. Add pauses with commas and periods. Use simple words. HeyGen's A.I. struggles with complex sentences and technical jargon. Write like you talk, not like you write an email. Test your script by reading it out loud first.

Third, create templates. Once you find an avatar and style that works, save it as a template. This lets you pump out consistent videos in minutes instead of choosing settings every time. Batch create five or ten videos in one session.

Here's the biggest mistake people make. They try to make the video too long. Keep it under two minutes. Shorter videos get watched. Longer videos get abandoned. If you have more to say, make it a series of short videos, not one long one.

Start with these basics and you'll be ahead of ninety percent of people who just use the defaults and wonder why their videos don't perform. The tool is powerful. Use it right.
