Someone jokingly asked if I could make a Trump State of the Union bingo card. Instead of just describing it, I built it. In one afternoon.

Here is what exists right now. A live website where anyone can pull up a randomized interactive bingo card. Every card is different. You can play one, two, or three cards at once. And when you hit five in a row, it explodes with "HUGE. THE BEST BINGO EVER."

But here is where it gets interesting. The card listens. Mac audio gets routed through a virtual device called BlackHole into a local A.I. called Whisper A.I., which transcribes the speech in real time. When it hears "tremendous," the square marks itself. When it hears "fake news," same thing. Every person watching on their phone sees their card update automatically through a home server. No cloud. No subscription. Just a local machine doing the work.

There is also a live countdown, a Trump self-mention counter, audio tones when keywords hit, timestamps on every marked square, and a ladybug button so players can message me directly.

One afternoon. No plan. No budget. A real thing, live on the internet, that people can play tonight.
