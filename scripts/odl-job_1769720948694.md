Here's the one A.I. tip that will actually change how you work. By the end of this video, you'll know the simple formula that turns A.I. from confusing to incredibly useful.

Most people ask A.I. terrible questions. They'll type something like "help me with marketing" and wonder why the response is useless. Here's what works instead. It's called the role plus task plus context formula.

First, give the A.I. a role. Say "You are a marketing expert" or "You are a writing coach." This tells the A.I. what perspective to take.

Second, give it a specific task. Instead of "help me with marketing," say "write a LinkedIn post" or "create an email sequence." Be exact about what you want.

Third, add context. Share the details that matter. For example, "I'm a small bakery owner launching gluten-free cookies for health-conscious customers."

Put it together and you get something like this. "You are a marketing expert. I need you to write a LinkedIn post about our new product launch. Here's the context. We're a small bakery launching gluten-free cookies for health-conscious customers."

See the difference? The first version gives you generic fluff. The second version gives you something you can actually use.

But here's the secret sauce. Don't expect perfection on the first try. A.I. works best when you iterate. Start with your role plus task plus context prompt. Then ask for changes. Say "make this more professional" or "add more technical details" or "create three variations of this."

Think of it like having a conversation with a really smart assistant. You wouldn't expect them to read your mind on the first try either.

Here's a real example. I asked ChatGPT to be a fitness coach and create a workout plan for someone with a busy schedule who works from home. The first response was okay but generic. Then I said "make this require no equipment and take exactly thirty minutes." Much better. Then I said "focus on exercises that help with back pain from sitting all day." Perfect.

The third key is to save your best prompts as templates. When you find a prompt that works well, don't lose it. Create a simple document with your go-to prompts for different situations.

For work emails, you might save "You are a professional communicator. Help me write an email that is direct but friendly. Here's the situation." Then you just fill in the specific details each time.

For learning new topics, try "You are an expert teacher. Explain this concept in simple terms with real-world examples. I'm a beginner in this area." Then add whatever you want to learn about.

For creative projects, use "You are a creative director. Help me brainstorm ideas for this project. Here's my goal and constraints." Then describe what you're working on.

The beauty of this approach is that it works with any A.I. tool. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot. They all respond better when you give them a clear role, specific task, and relevant context.

One last thing. Always fact-check important information. A.I. is incredibly helpful but it's not perfect. Think of it as a smart first draft, not the final answer.
