The way we log into things is about to change forever.

Okta's CEO Todd McKinnon just made a massive bet on something called AI agent identity. And if you're not paying attention, you should be.

Here's the problem. AI agents are starting to do real work. Not just answering questions. They're booking travel, accessing company databases, making purchases, and signing contracts on your behalf.

But right now, these agents are logging in with your credentials. Your username. Your password. Your identity.

That's a security nightmare.

McKinnon's vision is simple. AI agents need their own verified identity. Not a shared login. Not a workaround. A real, verifiable credential that says this AI is authorized to act for this person, at this company, for this specific task.

Think of it like giving your assistant their own badge instead of lending them yours.

Okta is building the infrastructure for this. And the timing is perfect. Every major tech company is deploying AI agents right now. Microsoft, Google, Salesforce, they all need a way to manage who these agents are and what they're allowed to do.

The old login model was built for humans clicking buttons. It was never designed for a world where half the accounts accessing your systems aren't even people.

If Okta gets this right, they become the gatekeeper for every AI agent in the enterprise. That's a trillion-dollar position.

And if they get it wrong? Well, that's how you end up with rogue AI agents buying things you never approved.

This is one of the biggest infrastructure plays in AI right now. Watch this space.
