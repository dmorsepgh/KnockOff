# Getting Started with ChatGPT for Business

[MUSIC: odl-jingle.mp3 | -12dB]

Want to actually get value from ChatGPT in your business? Here's how to start.

First, pay for it. The free version uses a weaker model. Twenty bucks a month gets you better reasoning, better analysis, and saves hours of rework. You'll make that back in your first few prompts.

Second, use the thinking models. Yes, they take sixty seconds instead of ten. But that extra wait gives you dramatically better outputs. Better summaries. Smarter recommendations. Less time fixing garbage answers.

Third, give it context. ChatGPT isn't a mind reader. Tell it your audience. Tell it your goals. Tell it your constraints. The more specific you are, the better your results.

Here's a quick example. Instead of asking "write me a marketing email," say "write a 200-word email for small business owners announcing our new scheduling feature, friendly tone, include a call to action."

See the difference? Context is everything.

Start with one use case. Maybe it's drafting emails. Maybe it's summarizing meeting notes. Maybe it's brainstorming product ideas. Pick one, get good at prompting for that, then expand.

And remember, A.I. is a tool, not a replacement. You still need to review, edit, and pick the good ideas from the bad ones.

Start small. Get specific. Build from there.
