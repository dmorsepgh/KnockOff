Here is something most people never think about when they start using A.I. tools. They assume you have to type everything. Every prompt, every instruction, every follow-up question, typed out word by word like it is 1997.

It is not 1997.

Your Mac has a dictation feature built right in, and when you combine it with Claude Code, something shifts. Instead of stopping what you are doing to type a question, you just talk. You describe the problem out loud. You say what you want. Claude hears it, processes it, and builds it. That is the loop. And once you feel it, you cannot go back.

I started calling this Claude Ears. Not an official feature, just a way of thinking about it. The idea that your A.I. assistant is listening, ready to respond, and you never have to break flow to type a command.

The way it works is simple. You enable dictation in your Mac settings, pick your hotkey, and now your voice becomes your keyboard. You are working in VS Code, you spot something wrong, you press the key and say what you see. Claude handles it.

Now here is the part nobody warns you about. Dictation does not know which window is active. If your cursor is in the wrong place, your words land in the code instead of the chat. I literally had the phrase "go back and get this for you" end up inside a shell script today. It broke the launch command. True story.

So the trick is simple. Click the Claude chat first. Then talk. Two seconds of attention that saves you twenty minutes of debugging.

This is what working with A.I. actually looks like. Not perfect. Not seamless. But faster than anything you have used before once you get the rhythm down.
