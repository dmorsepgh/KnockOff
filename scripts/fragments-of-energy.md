Here's something most productivity systems get completely wrong.

They ask you to plan your time. Schedule it. Block it out. Color code it like a kindergarten art project. And then at the end of the week you look at your calendar and think, that's cute. That's not what happened.

Fragments of Energy fixes that. You get a grid, 24 hours tall, 7 days wide, and every hour you log what you actually did. Not what you planned. What actually happened.

After a week of honest logging, patterns show up you didn't know were there. The hours that vanish into email. The admin work that owns your mornings. The scrolling that fills every gap like water.

And here's where it gets good. Every block that feels repetitive or manual, you flag it as an automation candidate. One click. At the end of the week the app packages your whole list into a prompt you drop straight into A.I. tools.

You went from "I'm busy all the time" to "here are twelve specific things I can hand off to A.I." That's the shift. From vague overwhelm to a concrete action list. In one week. With one grid.

Stop guessing where your time goes. Start seeing it.
