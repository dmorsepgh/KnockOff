# Daily Output — Zero-Input A.I.

Here's something that shifted how I think about A.I. completely.

Every tool follows the same three steps. Input, A.I., output. And the assumption is you have to show up first. Type the thing, wait for the thing.

But what if you didn't?

I was having a conversation today and I said, what if we eliminated the input entirely? Not simplified it, removed it. What if A.I. just knew enough about your work that it could skip straight to the output?

So we built it. Right there, in about 18 minutes.

Every morning at 7 A.M., a script wakes up on my Mac. No alarm, no prompt from me. It looks at what I worked on the day before, the commits, the files I touched. It hands that to Claude. Claude writes me a ready-to-record YouTube script. Copies it to my clipboard. Texts me.

I wake up, grab my coffee, my phone buzzes, and the script is already there.

Today it wrote a video about a day when I did nothing. Zero commits. And it turned that into something about momentum, and resistance, and why the blank day is the setup for tomorrow.

This is what zero-input A.I. looks like. Not science fiction. A shell script, a cron job, and Claude running while you sleep.

The input was never the creative part anyway.
